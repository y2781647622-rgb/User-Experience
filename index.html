Problem Statement

“ By 2040, four-day work weeks mean more life happening in HDB neighborhoods—but also more delivery robots moving through them. ”

In narrow walkways and void decks, everyday routines are disrupted by congestion, safety concerns, and rising tension between people and machines. The challenge isn't just logistics—it's preserving the rhythm of human life.

Research & Key Insights

Active Mobility & Transport (2030–2040)

  • Cycling network: 730km → ~1,300km by 2030 (LTA).
  • Footpath width: 1.5m clear; Cycling path: 2.0m.
  • 20-min towns: Amenities ≤ 20 min walk/cycle.
  • 45-min city: 90% peak journeys < 45 min.

Delivery Robots (Autonomous)

  • Specs: Knee-high, 6-wheeled, 5–8 km/h, 30 lbs cargo.
  • Stats: Starship (9M+ deliveries), Serve Robotics (Uber Eats).
  • Market: $0.8B (2025) → $3.24B (2030).
  • Safety: Obstructions & accessibility issues.

Culture Analysis

Cultural Truths

  • Singaporeans value order, predictability, and punctuality — ambiguity is not tolerated.
  • Complaining ≠ rejection → it is a signal that standards are not met.
  • High trust in systems only when:
    • Rules are visible
    • Enforcement exists
    • Behaviour is legible
  • Automation is not rejected morally — it is rejected when it violates shared spatial etiquette.

“Because most things run smoothly, we definitely work with the best-case scenario and any deviation from that might unsettle us. We don’t “live with” or “tolerate” anything that has not been adequately managed or implemented. And while this may come across as pettiness etc to some, I think it does exemplify a respect for high standards. In that sense, a little Japanese.”
